🌿 Hemp — Virginia + federal

No rule change in last 48h. Standing Aug 15 VA obligation and federal §781 status carried for their countdowns.
Action Required

VA: >2 mg total THC per package barred from hemp sale beginning Aug 15, 2026

Standing obligation (previously announced; carried for its imminent deadline — ~23 days out). Effective August 15, 2026, HB 30 eliminates the “25:1 CBD:THC ratio” and a product with more than two milligrams of total THC per package cannot be produced or sold as a hemp product in Virginia. Virginia hemp operators must audit inventory and stop selling / relabel / reformulate any package exceeding 2 mg total THC before that date. No change to this rule in the last 48h.

Federal hemp ban status — §781 still on track for Nov 12, 2026; delay/repeal bills pending, none passed

Watch item: federal hemp ban status. P.L. 119-37 §781 (redefining hemp to exclude finished products above ~0.4 mg total THC per container) remains scheduled to take effect November 12, 2026. Delay/repeal vehicles — the Hemp Planting Predictability Act (H.R. 7024 / S. 3686), the Mace bill / American Hemp Protection Act, and Barr’s Lawful Hemp Protection Act — remain introduced but unpassed; no floor action, appropriations rider, or Farm Bill amendment advanced in the last 48 hours. Plan as if §781 takes effect on schedule.

Informational

Hemp oversight transferring VDACS → CCA in August 2026

HB 30 moves the Office of Hemp Enforcement’s responsibilities for regulated hemp products from VDACS to the CCA, anticipated in August 2026. Current Hemp Product Retail Facility Registrations remain valid until their expiration; renewal / new-application instructions are to be posted on the CCA site as they become available. Registered retailers and industrial-hemp growers are to receive direct communication from VDACS.
